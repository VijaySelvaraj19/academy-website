AUTOMOTIVE EMBEDDED SYSTEM

Automotive embedded system is the backbone of modern vehicles to make them more efficient, safety (Brake system, Airbag system, Electronic Stability Program), driver assistance systems (Navigation, night vision, blind spot detection) or to comply with legal necessities (reducing the emission of pollutants by intelligent engine control), comfy and secure. An increase in the complexity of modern cars also increase the demands overwhelmed on diagnostics, maintenance and repair. Currently, there is a noticeable shortage of qualified automotive technicians with excellent skills. Hence there is the need for a practical training program in automotive electronics with emphasis on Embedded System of Vehicle.The main objective of this course is to provide an understanding of the technology essential to the design and implementation of an embedded system using suitable hardware and software tools for automotive application.

Introduction to Embedded Systems: Understanding the basics of embedded systems, their components, and their role in automotive applications.
Microcontrollers and Microprocessors: Learning about different types of microcontrollers and processors used in automotive systems, their architecture, and programming.
Sensors and Actuators: Exploring various sensors (e.g., temperature, pressure, proximity) and actuators (e.g., motors, solenoids) crucial for automotive control and monitoring.

Communication Protocols:Delving into protocols like CAN, LIN, Ethernet, and FlexRay used for inter-component communication within vehicles.
ECU Development: Understanding Electronic Control Units (ECUs), their role in automotive systems, and their development process.
Software Development: Learning about real-time operating systems, firmware development, and software design for reliable and efficient automotive applications.

Functional Safety (ISO 26262): Exploring safety concepts, hazard analysis, and safety mechanisms required for automotive systems compliant with safety standards.
AUTOSAR Framework: Understanding the AUTOSAR architecture, methodology, and its application in developing standardized automotive software.
Embedded Diagnostics: Learning about fault detection, diagnosis, and error handling mechanisms critical for maintaining vehicle performance and reliability.
